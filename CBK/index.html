FAQ

Questions you
might have

Straight answers to the things most people want to know before reaching out.

What exactly does a bookkeeper do?
Think of bookkeeping as organizing and maintaining the financial story of your business. A bookkeeper records and categorizes transactions, reconciles accounts, tracks income and expenses, and ensures your financial records are accurate and up to date. Good bookkeeping helps you understand how your business is performing and makes tax preparation significantly easier. Services may include:
  • Monthly bookkeeping and account reconciliation
  • Financial reporting (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow)
  • Accounts payable and receivable management
  • Cleanup and catch-up bookkeeping
  • Sales tax reporting and filing
  • QuickBooks Online setup, review, and training
  • Payroll through QuickBooks Payroll
How does working with a bookkeeper actually work?
I work exclusively with QuickBooks Online. QuickBooks allows business owners and bookkeepers to collaborate securely without sharing passwords or bank logins. Through QuickBooks Online's accountant access, I can work on your books while you maintain full control of your accounts and sensitive information. If you already use QuickBooks Online, I'll guide you through granting access. If not, we'll find the right path forward together during our first conversation.
Are you going to judge me?
Absolutely not. One of the most common things I hear is some version of "I know my books are a mess." Please know you are not alone. Business owners are busy. Life happens. Records get behind. Accounts get mixed. Software gets set up incorrectly. These situations are far more common than you might think. My job is not to judge where you are — my job is to help you move forward. Whether you're a few weeks behind or a few years behind, we'll start where you are and create a plan together.
How long will it take to get things straightened out?
Every business is different. Simple projects may move quickly, while businesses with months or years of backlog naturally take longer. In most cases, it takes several months to fully clean up, organize, and optimize bookkeeping systems. I believe in doing the work carefully and correctly rather than rushing through it. My goal is to create books you can rely on — not just books that look complete.
Why is it a good idea to outsource bookkeeping?
Most business owners didn't start their business because they love bookkeeping. When it continually gets pushed to the bottom of the to-do list, it creates stress, uncertainty, and costly mistakes. Outsourcing gives you back your time, reduces mental clutter, helps you make better decisions, keeps you compliant with payroll and sales tax requirements, and prepares you for tax season without the scramble. Many clients tell me the biggest benefit isn't just accurate books — it's the peace of mind.
